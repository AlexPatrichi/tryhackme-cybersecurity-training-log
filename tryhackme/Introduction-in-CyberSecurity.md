## Objective  

Gain a foundational understanding of cybersecurity, including:  
- Differences between offensive and defensive security  
- Common roles and career paths in cybersecurity  
- Overview of practical tasks in SOC and ethical hacking  

## Key Concepts Learned  

1. Offensive Security:  
- Ethical hacking in a controlled environment  
- Simulating attacks to understand vulnerabilities  
- Importance of legality and responsible disclosure  

2. Defensive Security:  
- Protecting systems from attacks  
- Threat Intelligence, SOC operations, DFIR, Malware Analysis, and SIEM tools  
- Monitoring, detection, and incident response  

3. Cybersecurity Careers:  
- Roles include SOC Analyst, Penetration Tester, Malware Analyst, Threat Intel Analyst  
- Each role requires a mix of technical skills and problem-solving

## Lessons Learned

- Cybersecurity requires a balance between **attacking and defending skills**
- Defensive roles (SOC, malware analysis, threat intel) require strong monitoring and analytical skills  
- Offensive exercises teach how vulnerabilities are exploited, which informs better defense  
- Understanding how Security Operations Centers monitor threats and how Digital Forensics & Incident Response investigates incidents prepares you for real-world cybersecurity work.  
- Early career exploration is important to set goals and learning paths
